UFC 324: GAETHJE VS. PIMBLETT. The Paramount era begins...
It could also be called "The Advertisement Era"

I paid around $100 for the premium annual subscription for Paramount+. My girlfriend asked me, "Are there going to be ads?" To which I replied by laughing. I didn't really read into what I get with my subscription, but I went for the higher tier one because I don't want to miss out on something UFC-related that may be behind a paywall. But the reason I laughed is because I was pretty certain they were going to run advertisements during the live broadcast no matter what tier I paid for. And guess what?

A D V E R T I S E M E N T S  E V E R Y W H E R E

Ever since the Fertittas sold the UFC, there has been a concentration on monetizing everything UFC. Of course there are in-octagon ads, commentary does ad reads, Buffer does ad reads, normal ads between fights. But as time goes on, more things are advertised: getting your name on the canvas, the UFC prep point is sponsored, I saw the headphones the commentators wear now have a sponsorship sticker, instead of showing walkouts they show an advertisement, advertisements between rounds. Now they have it where even if they *do* show the fighters between the corners, an L-frame ad shows up and you can't hear the corner audio anyway. During this event, the Ghostface killer from **SCREAM** was shown walking around behind fighters (Waldo Acosta specifically) and that led into a trailer for *Scream*. Massive amount of ads and I do not see that changing.

To me this was expected—maybe not for most of the main card like they did, but still. The amount of money Paramount paid for this partnership was astronomical, so they are going to monetize everything. Dana White was asked about this and he said he'll look into it and he is sure things will change, but that's not really up to Dana White. What I think Paramount will do is try to integrate the advertisements less intrusively. Rather than cutting to an ad and not seeing anything, they'll show the ad side by side to the content we want to see. But will the audio from the ad dominate, or the audio from the walkout or corner? My guess is the advertising.

This is why I'm calling it the advertisement era. We are going to see how advertising is approached, evolves, and gets implemented into a live sporting event without feeling intrusive. It's gonna feel like the Truman Show.

As I'm writing this I'm seeing that ads were trending on Twitter, too. It reads: "FANS SLAM ADS DURING UFC 324 DEBUT ON PARAMOUNT+"

Listen, MMA fans (really UFC or betting fans) are ultra shitty in general. They are 87% fairweather fans and I would truly believe that statistic even though I have zero data. But their complaints about ads are justified, and I only saw a couple of bootlickers say they didn't mind.

OK — so TONS of advertising. The next takeaway I had was "BLINK 182".
Travis Barker's involvement with MMA goes back to his fandom, but also a business opportunity for his clothing company Famous Stars and Straps. He sponsored fighters back in the day. Anyways, his and Blink 182's presence was featured heavily—from their music, to Travis interviews, to Travis playing drums in the octagon (not live but on some video), to him in the crowd with Kourtney... it was a lot. Too much.

There was a lot of filler at the beginning of the broadcast as well because quite a few fights fell out, so I'm sure they were prepping for a much longer run time that was filled with fighting and not talking. Michael Johnson vs. Alexander Hernandez, Cam Smotherman, Harrison vs. Nunes. This was less of an issue for me, but it did piss a lot of the fairweather fans off but... they'll complain about literally everything, including a good fight.

Dominick Cruz was inducted into the Hall of Fame which is very much deserved, and he was one of my favorite fighters when he was active... well, as active as he could be given the injuries. But he is a style I still like to study.

When the main card started, they did a really good promo package to kick off the new era.

7 right, 4 wrong. My K/D ratio is 1.75.

At the very last second I changed my pick from Natalia Silva to Rose Namajunas, and that happens to be the fight I disagreed with the scorecards on. I think Rose won the first two rounds and Natalia obviously took the third. I also see an argument for Andrey Pulyaev defeating Ateba Gautier, but I'd need to rewatch it. My dog was going insane and I missed chunks of the fight.

I enjoyed Ty Miller's clean, straight punches. He had some difficulty against a southpaw in Adam Fugitt, but he did well. Adam performed well given my expectations and I thought he had excellent kicks to the body.

Josh Hokit is a better fighter and much better athlete than Denzel Freeman. Freeman had nothing—covering up and barely countering the onslaught. I think he may have landed a couple shots, but he didn't keep that going. Hokit's power wasn't impressive in this fight, but I think as he gets more comfortable standing, he'll be able to see his openings better. Right now, it's all pressure and volume. He'll need composure eventually, otherwise he'll get sniped. I still despise the guy's absolutely stupid WWE gimmick which is just him impersonating wrestlers and Chael Sonnen... verbatim, might I add. His only original thing is to say 1 controversial thing. It's stupid and if the guy wasn't an athlete, he'd be the first to sign up for ICE.

Moving on to Arnold Allen. I really thought Arnold would win. He had some real crispy striking and some good movement, but eventually, Jean's power fucked him up and Arnold also gassed out. Jean goes on and I don't. He's a bit of a donk, insane, maybe acoustic, emotional, but he's fun to watch. If one were to do absurdist, stereotypical comedy on a Brazilian MMA fighter, you would come up with Jean Silva.

Derrick Lewis and Waldo were a let down. Waldo just waited and capitalized on Derrick making mistakes. Derrick didn't want to make too many mistakes, but he did... Waldo is good, not great. Moves OK. He is a different name in the heavyweight division and will continue to be in interesting matchups. I need to rewatch more of his fights because I haven't come to a conclusion on how I feel about him.

Sean O'Malley wins a decision. Some are saying Song Yadong was robbed. I disagree. I could see it being a controversial decision as in it could go either way, but I felt Sean won. Comes down to the first round. Sean landed the cleaner, better strikes at a higher volume in round 1. Stats back that up. I'm not big on looking at stats and making a decision, but in this instance it totally makes sense. You look at the first round, you see O'Malley landing at a higher clip and see the damage on Song's face. What sways people is Song's explosivity and power, but one or two big moments can't eclipse who did the real cumulative damage. Sean wasn't super hurt by the end of that round and neither was Song. But Song had visible damage. The numbers don't lie on this one.

Justin Gaethje and Paddy Pimblett. This was a tough one to pick for me personally for a few reasons.
1. Both fighters get hit often.
2. Justin Gaethje is 37 in the lightweight division.
3. Paddy is younger but, skill-wise, not the better fighter.
4. ...It's Gaethje. He could be the best fighter on planet earth and still lose cuz he is chaos.

Ultimately, I went with Gaethje because I thought he'd have better cardio over five rounds, he is the better fighter, and I didn't think he'd get taken down. There is the pressure of the moment, aura, and if Gaethje is just gonna look old.

The only way I can describe this fight is that these two BEAT THE SHIT OUT OF EACH OTHER. People want to complain about the eye poke that Gaethje did and nut shots... Even if those didn't happen, Paddy was going to get fucked up regardless. They both really beat the shit out of each other, but Gaethje definitely put it on Paddy more. They both left something in the cage. Especially Paddy. Gaethje did well to try to exhaust Paddy as much as he could. Those early rounds, Gaethje didn't do too much in terms of volume of offense. He just let Paddy throw bombs. But kudos to Paddy who did not slow down as much as I thought he would, and the dude can really take a punch. I don't know how his chin will fare after this fight, but let me just tell you that he has a fucking chin. He took 25 minutes worth of absolute bombs.

Although an entertaining fight... I don't see either of them beating Arman or Ilia. It was an entertaining main event in terms of action, but it misses the mark of the true best in the world and what was at stake. It was an interim title... that's why I scored it a 9/10. I think it would be more fair to put an 8/10... we need better storyline, stakes, and skill level for the 9 and 10 marks.

Nonetheless. About 60% of the card was a good time.
