    <h1>UFC Freedom 250 at the White House: Full Card Preview and Predictions</h1>
  </header>

  <p>
    UFC Freedom 250 takes place this Sunday at one of the most unusual venues
    in combat sports history: the White House lawn.
  </p>

  <p>
    The event was announced nearly a year ago, and after months of political
    arguments, speculation, complaints, and terrible takes from every possible
    direction, it is finally here.
  </p>

  <p>
    Personally, I find the political affiliation surrounding the UFC incredibly
    annoying and goofy. That will be especially true if Kid Rock becomes involved.
  </p>

  <p>
    More than anything, though, the event leaves me wondering why the UFC has not
    taken more chances on large or unconventional venues in the past.
  </p>

  <p>
    Conor McGregor fighting at Croke Park was discussed for years, but Dana White
    did not want to deal with the weather, insects, and other logistical risks.
    AT&amp;T Stadium, home of the Dallas Cowboys, has also been suggested. White
    has said he believes the UFC could sell it out, but he prefers the atmosphere
    of indoor arenas.
  </p>

  <p>
    At the same time, the UFC was willing to hold an event at the Sphere in Las
    Vegas. That show was impressive, even if the experience was also a little
    strange.
  </p>

  <p>
    The promotion has never held an event in Hawaii, either. That is remarkable
    for a state that produced fighters such as BJ Penn and Max Holloway.
  </p>

  <p>
    From a production standpoint, however, I appreciate what the UFC is attempting
    with Freedom 250. Staging an MMA event on the White House lawn is an
    extraordinary logistical undertaking, and I am genuinely curious to see how
    the presentation looks.
  </p>

  <p>
    I am less excited about the national anthem, ceremonies, and whatever other
    pomp surrounds the fights. Boxing frequently turns major events into exhausting
    spectacles, with elaborate entrances and ceremonies delaying the main event
    for what feels like hours.
  </p>

  <p>
    The UFC was originally built around the idea that it would not make the same
    mistakes as boxing, so it will be interesting to see how the company balances
    the historic setting with the actual fights.
  </p>

  <p>
    Whatever happens, Freedom 250 will bring enormous exposure to the UFC. It will
    also bring opinions from people who rarely, if ever, watch MMA.
  </p>

  <p>
    That is the inevitable result of a major spectacle. New viewers arrive,
    attention increases, and suddenly everyone has a fully formed opinion about a
    sport they discovered five minutes ago.
  </p>

  <p>
    Regular MMA fans already produce enough questionable analysis, so prepare
    yourselves.
  </p>

  <p>
    New viewers may also discover that MMA can look remarkably homoerotic at
    times. That is simply part of the experience.
  </p>

  <p>
    With all the ceremony and political discussion out of the way, let us move to
    the subject that has received surprisingly little attention: the fights.
  </p>

  <section>
    <h2>Ilia Topuria vs. Justin Gaethje</h2>

    <p>
      The main event features Ilia Topuria against Justin Gaethje in what is being
      promoted as a lightweight title unification bout.
    </p>

    <p>
      The title situation does not make much sense. If the UFC is going to
      distribute championships as freely as it often does, Topuria should probably
      also possess the BMF title, while Gaethje should be attempting to unify the
      lightweight championship and regain the BMF belt at the same time.
    </p>

    <p>
      Topuria enters as a substantial favorite, which is understandable. He has
      looked exceptional and is the considerably younger fighter. Topuria is 29
      years old, while Gaethje is 37.
    </p>

    <p>
      Gaethje is taller at 5 feet 11 inches compared with Topuria at 5 feet 7
      inches, but his reach advantage is only one inch.
    </p>

    <p>My pick is Topuria.</p>

    <p>
      One of the most important technical elements will be how Topuria handles
      Gaethje's single-collar tie. Gaethje uses it constantly to break his
      opponent's posture before throwing heavy punches at close range.
    </p>

    <p>
      Leg kicks will also be significant. Gaethje often throws them from unusually
      close distances, which could be interesting against Topuria's more
      conventional boxing stance.
    </p>

    <p>
      Topuria is generally effective at shifting his weight between his lead and
      rear legs, but Gaethje's timing and willingness to exchange could still
      create problems.
    </p>

    <p>
      Gaethje is extremely comfortable in chaotic close-range exchanges. He has
      even thrown wild head kicks from the clinch. That particular technique may
      not determine this fight, but it illustrates how unpredictable he can be
      when the distance collapses.
    </p>

    <p>
      Topuria does a good job slipping punches and protecting himself behind his
      shoulder. However, he can be hit on his right side, particularly by hooks
      and kicks that travel around his shoulder rather than directly through his
      guard.
    </p>

    <p>
      It does not happen constantly, but it happens often enough to be worth
      watching.
    </p>

    <p>
      Conditioning may also become a major factor. This fight could be contested
      at a high pace and become ugly very quickly.
    </p>

    <p>
      The outdoor setting adds another variable. The canvas could become slippery,
      particularly with the enormous number of sponsor logos usually printed
      across it.
    </p>

    <p>
      By the time the main event begins, the surface may also be covered with
      sweat, blood, and water from the previous fights. It will be interesting to
      see whether the UFC replaces or cleans the canvas before the main event.
    </p>

    <p>
      There has also been discussion about the pressure associated with competing
      on this card. Gaethje may feel a particular obligation to perform for the
      American crowd and the dignitaries in attendance, especially with a
      championship at stake.
    </p>

    <p>
      Gaethje has historically thrived under pressure, though, and he appears to
      enjoy being the underdog. That psychological element may work in his favor.
    </p>

    <p>
      Topuria has reportedly predicted a first-round knockout. I believe he wins,
      but I expect the finish to come in the second or third round. I am accounting
      for the outdoor conditions and the possibility of a more cautious opening
      than expected.
    </p>

    <p>
      This is an excellent main event, even though I still believe Arman Tsarukyan
      should be occupying this position.
    </p>

    <p>
      <strong>
        Prediction: Ilia Topuria by knockout in Round 2 or Round 3.
      </strong>
    </p>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h2>Alex Pereira vs. Ciryl Gane</h2>

    <p>
      In the co-main event, Alex "Poatan" Pereira moves to heavyweight to face
      Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight championship.
    </p>

    <p>
      The betting odds are close, and it will be worth monitoring how the line
      moves as the fight approaches.
    </p>

    <p>
      Gane is two years younger and holds a slight reach advantage, measuring 81
      inches compared with Pereira's 79. Gane is also the natural heavyweight,
      although the two fighters have relatively similar professional records.
    </p>

    <p>
      Gane's game is built around mobility. He uses extensive lateral movement,
      attacks the body with kicks, and generally manages distance well.
    </p>

    <p>
      However, he sometimes retreats in straight lines and frequently carries his
      hands low.
    </p>

    <p>
      Pereira will likely attempt to take away Gane's space, cut off the cage, and
      force him behind the outer boundary line. From there, Pereira can attack as
      Gane circles away or catch him during exchanges when the pressure begins to
      build.
    </p>

    <p>
      Pereira is fully capable of scoring a knockout, but I am leaning toward Gane.
    </p>

    <p>
      Gane is the more dynamic overall fighter, and that versatility matters at
      heavyweight. I believe it will take more than one clean punch to remove him
      from the fight.
    </p>

    <p>
      Over five rounds, Gane may gradually establish his rhythm and accumulate
      momentum.
    </p>

    <p>
      I am also interested in whether Gane attempts any takedowns. The clinch
      exchanges and positions against the cage could be pivotal.
    </p>

    <p>
      Whichever fighter lands the cleaner shots as they separate may gain a major
      advantage.
    </p>

    <p>
      A decision victory for Gane is entirely possible, but in the spirit of
      America and freedom, let us hope the fight ends inside the distance.
    </p>

    <p>
      I expect Gane to accumulate damage and eventually land something devastating
      from range in the third round. Still, this is heavyweight MMA, so no result
      should be considered surprising.
    </p>

    <p>
      This may be the fight I am anticipating most, partly because Pereira's
      walkout is one of the best in combat sports. Hearing Sepultura played at the
      White House would be undeniably cold.
    </p>

    <p>
      <strong>
        Prediction: Ciryl Gane by knockout or technical knockout in Round 3.
      </strong>
    </p>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h2>Sean O'Malley vs. Aiemann Zahabi</h2>

    <p>
      Sean O'Malley enters his fight against Aiemann Zahabi as a substantial
      favorite.
    </p>

    <p>
      The size of the betting line initially seems surprising because Zahabi is a
      durable and intelligent fighter. It is not impossible to imagine him turning
      the contest into an ugly, competitive fight and winning a decision.
    </p>

    <p>
      There is also the completely scientific concern that O'Malley could slip on
      the outdoor canvas and injure one of his famously delicate ankles.
    </p>

    <p>
      The odds become more understandable after examining the matchup. O'Malley has
      the greater name recognition and ranking, and he is younger, taller, and
      longer.
    </p>

    <p>
      Statistically, Zahabi tends to absorb roughly one strike for every strike he
      lands. O'Malley, by comparison, is much better at landing without remaining
      in position for the immediate return.
    </p>

    <p>
      Zahabi and his brother, Firas, are capable of constructing an effective game
      plan, so I would not completely dismiss his chances. He may be able to
      disrupt O'Malley's rhythm and make the fight uncomfortable.
    </p>

    <p>
      Ultimately, however, I expect O'Malley's length, movement, and defensive
      awareness to carry him to a decision victory.
    </p>

    <p><strong>Prediction: Sean O'Malley by decision.</strong></p>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h2>Derrick Lewis vs. Josh Hokit</h2>

    <p>
      Donald Trump's favorite fighter, Derrick Lewis, faces Josh Hokit, who may
      replace Lewis as Trump's favorite fighter by the end of Sunday night.
    </p>

    <p>
      Lewis is 41 years old, while Hokit is 28. Hokit is reportedly only a few
      inches shorter, but he gives up approximately five inches in reach.
    </p>

    <p>Despite that disadvantage, Hokit enters as the favorite.</p>

    <p>
      There is always a reasonable argument for betting on Lewis by knockout. He
      is one of the most powerful fighters in UFC history, owns an enormous number
      of knockout victories, and competes in a division where one punch can erase
      everything that happened beforehand.
    </p>

    <p>
      Hokit also absorbed plenty of punches in his fight with Curtis Blaydes, so
      Lewis will have opportunities.
    </p>

    <p>
      However, this matchup reminds me of how easily Daniel Cormier was able to
      take Lewis down. Hokit does not possess the same credentials as Cormier, but
      the comparison is relevant enough to illustrate the potential problem.
    </p>

    <p>
      Lewis may land a few heavy punches or kicks, but I expect Hokit to attack the
      legs, drag him to the canvas, and control the fight from there.
    </p>

    <p>
      Once the fight reaches the ground, Hokit should be able to produce damage
      and find a submission. The only question is whether it comes through an arm
      triangle or a rear-naked choke.
    </p>

    <p>
      The rear-naked choke may sound a little too homoerotic for all the new
      viewers, but an arm triangle looks like an awkward hug, so there is no
      perfect solution.
    </p>

    <p>I will go with the traditional option.</p>

    <p>
      <strong>
        Prediction: Josh Hokit by rear-naked choke in Round 1.
      </strong>
    </p>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h2>Michael Chandler vs. Mauricio Ruffy</h2>

    <p>
      Michael Chandler wants to be Donald Trump's favorite fighter, but he is not.
      He also wants to fight Conor McGregor, but apparently that is not happening
      either.
    </p>

    <p>
      Instead, Chandler faces Mauricio Ruffy, who is at least vaguely reminiscent
      of McGregor.
    </p>

    <p>
      Ruffy is a major favorite, and it is easy to understand why. Chandler is 40
      years old, while Ruffy is 29. Ruffy is also taller and owns a meaningful
      reach advantage.
    </p>

    <p>
      As long as Ruffy does not spend three rounds being controlled by Chandler's
      wrestling—and does not get kicked directly in the face—I have difficulty
      seeing him lose.
    </p>

    <p>
      Ruffy has reportedly built a gym and training camp around himself rather
      than operating as a conventional member of the Fighting Nerds team.
    </p>

    <p>
      I like that approach. It resembles the individualized camps once constructed
      around major boxing stars such as Muhammad Ali and George Foreman.
    </p>

    <p>
      There are faint shades of McGregor against Chad Mendes in this matchup,
      although the similarities begin to disappear as soon as you examine them
      closely.
    </p>

    <p>
      Chandler should have moments early, especially if he commits to his wrestling
      and refuses to engage recklessly.
    </p>

    <p>
      History suggests that he will eventually abandon the safest strategy and
      enter a chaotic exchange.
    </p>

    <p>
      Unless Chandler has substantially increased his dosage of American-made
      freedom supplements, I expect Ruffy to finish him.
    </p>

    <p>
      <strong>
        Prediction: Mauricio Ruffy by technical knockout in Round 2.
      </strong>
    </p>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h2>Bo Nickal vs. Kyle Daukaus</h2>

    <p>
      The card also includes another candidate for Donald Trump's favorite
      fighter: Bo Nickal.
    </p>

    <p>
      Nickal faces Kyle Daukaus in a matchup between two southpaws. The last time
      Nickal fought a southpaw, Reinier de Ridder repeatedly found him with strikes
      and eventually finished him.
    </p>

    <p>That experience is relevant here.</p>

    <p>
      Nickal enters as a moderate favorite. His record stands at 8-1, while
      Daukaus brings considerably more experience with a 17-4 record and is
      beginning his second UFC stint.
    </p>

    <p>
      Daukaus is a few inches taller, although their reach is identical. Nickal is
      three years younger at 30.
    </p>

    <p>I like Daukaus more than the betting odds suggest.</p>

    <p>
      The most likely outcome is still Nickal controlling the fight through
      takedowns, mat returns, and superior positioning before winning a decision.
    </p>

    <p>
      He is the more accomplished athlete and possesses vastly superior wrestling
      credentials.
    </p>

    <p>
      Nickal's striking has improved, but he still needs experience. He looked
      solid against Rodolfo Vieira, although Vieira offered almost nothing
      offensively in that fight.
    </p>

    <p>He moved with the urgency of a parked car.</p>

    <p>
      Daukaus has the tools to make this contest ugly. He can occupy space,
      pressure forward, and land combinations. He will probably absorb punches in
      return, but he may be durable enough to take them.
    </p>

    <p>
      Nickal's defensive striking and footwork are not yet developed enough to
      completely neutralize Daukaus. When exchanges become uncomfortable, Nickal
      will almost certainly search for takedowns.
    </p>

    <p>
      Daukaus may not possess elite takedown defense, and he will probably lose
      certain scrambling positions. However, he is a legitimate submission threat.
    </p>

    <p>
      Pressure could also influence Nickal's decision-making. He will not want to
      look bad in front of his powerful golfing friend.
    </p>

    <p>
      That may encourage him to attempt risky techniques that do not serve his
      best interests, much as he did against de Ridder.
    </p>

    <p>
      The correct strategy for Nickal is straightforward: wrestle constantly,
      establish dominant positions, produce damage, and continue wrestling.
    </p>

    <p>
      This is one of the best and most logical matchups on the card. Picking the
      underdog may not be the intelligent choice, but I am willing to have some
      fun.
    </p>

    <p>
      I believe Daukaus can hurt Nickal on the feet, force a desperate wrestling
      exchange, and attack his neck during the transition.
    </p>

    <p>
      <strong>
        Prediction: Kyle Daukaus by submission in Round 2.
      </strong>
    </p>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h2>Diego Lopes vs. Steve Garcia</h2>

    <p>
      The opening fight of the White House card features Diego Lopes against Steve
      Garcia.
    </p>

    <p>
      It is easy to understand why the UFC selected this matchup to begin the
      event. Conceptually, it should produce immediate fireworks.
    </p>

    <p>
      Garcia is only a slight underdog. He is three years older, slightly taller,
      and appears to hold a reach advantage of approximately two and a half inches.
    </p>

    <p>
      Garcia owns a 19-5 record, while Lopes enters at 27-8.
    </p>

    <p>
      The southpaw-versus-orthodox dynamic also makes the fight interesting, with
      Garcia operating from the southpaw stance.
    </p>

    <p>
      Garcia is unusually built for a featherweight. His limbs look exceptionally
      long, and his style is awkward in an effective way.
    </p>

    <p>
      He uses constant movement and feints, maintains an active lead hand, and
      frequently attacks with front kicks.
    </p>

    <p>
      I am a fan of his approach. His volume and movement alone could create
      problems for Lopes.
    </p>

    <p>
      However, Lopes' losses have generally come against elite competition. His
      difficulty cutting off the cage is well established, but that weakness may
      be less important against Garcia, who is more willing to engage.
    </p>

    <p>
      Garcia manages long range effectively, but he can be caught by counters.
    </p>

    <p>
      His striking differential is remarkable, averaging 5.39 significant strikes
      landed per minute while absorbing only 2.12.
    </p>

    <p>
      Those numbers should be viewed in the context of his shorter average fight
      time and the level of opposition he has faced, but they remain impressive.
    </p>

    <p>
      Lopes' durability, experience, and power should be major factors. He is also
      the better grappler on paper, although Garcia's unusual style could make the
      grappling exchanges less predictable than expected.
    </p>

    <p>
      I believe the fight reaches the scorecards, with damage serving as the
      deciding factor in Lopes' favor.
    </p>

    <p>
      Regardless of the result, this is an excellent matchup and an ideal way to
      start the event.
    </p>

    <p><strong>Prediction: Diego Lopes by decision.</strong></p>

  <section>
    <h2>Final Thoughts</h2>

    <p>UFC Freedom 250 is unquestionably a historic event.</p>

    <p>
      Whether the political theater enhances or distracts from the fights remains
      to be seen, but the White House setting guarantees that the show will attract
      an enormous audience.
    </p>

    <p>
      It will bring longtime fans, first-time viewers, dignitaries, celebrities,
      political arguments, bad analysis, and possibly Kid Rock together for one
      strange night of cage fighting.
    </p>

    <p>
      Once it is over, everyone can finally stop complaining about it and begin
      preparing for the next unavoidable spectacle: the return of Conor McGregor.
    </p>

    <p>Yay.</p>
